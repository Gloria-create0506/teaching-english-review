Find Evidence · 原文定位

答案不靠猜，回原文找证据

“What's wrong with our swords and spears?” asked the gibbon. Wukong said they were fine for normal monkeys and apes, but he was very powerful and needed a much heavier weapon. The gibbon suggested that a dragon king at the bottom of the Eastern Sea might have one.

Wukong said a quick spell so he could breathe underwater. Then he dived into the stream, swam to the ocean, and went deeper and deeper. Soon he arrived at a large palace where the Dragon King and the Dragon Queen were having tea.

Wukong told them he was king of the Fruit and Flower Mountain. He did not want tea. He wanted a heavy weapon because he was as strong as a god. The Dragon King asked a turtle soldier to find one for Sun Wukong.

The turtle soldier dragged in a huge sword and fell down, out of breath. Wukong picked it up with no trouble at all, waved it through the air, and said the sword was too light. The Dragon Queen whispered that the monkey was rude, but Wukong still wanted something better.

A few minutes later, several turtles and octopuses dragged an enormous spear into the room. Wukong lifted it and stabbed at the air, but he shook his head again. The spear was still too light. Wukong stomped his foot and said he was not leaving until he got what he wanted.

The Dragon Queen reminded her husband about the magical iron bar in the treasure. The Dragon King sighed and led Wukong to the treasure room. A huge iron pillar stood in the center, shining with golden light. “If you can pick it up,” said the Dragon King, “you can have it.”
